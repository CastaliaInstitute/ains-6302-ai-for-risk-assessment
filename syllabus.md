# Syllabus

## Course Description

`AINS6302 AI for Risk Assessment` is part of the Cybersecurity AI specialization
(AINS6300–AINS6302) in the Aurnova MSAI program.

Teaches AI-based risk modeling and predictive analysis for cybersecurity decision-making. Covers quantitative risk (FAIR), vulnerability prioritization, attack-path analysis on graphs, and forecasting breach likelihood under uncertainty.

## Prerequisites

- AINS6001 Foundations of Artificial Intelligence
- AINS6002 Machine Learning & Predictive Modeling
- Working comfort with Python, Git, and the command line

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of the course, students should be able to:

- articulate and apply the concepts of Module 1: quantitative risk and vulnerability prioritization
- articulate and apply the concepts of Module 2: attack-path modeling and breach forecasting
- evaluate the security, operational, and ethical trade-offs of
  deploying AI systems in a cybersecurity context
- communicate findings to both technical and executive audiences

## Modules

- Module 1: Quantitative Risk and Vulnerability Prioritization
- Module 2: Attack-Path Modeling and Breach Forecasting

## Assessment Pattern

- weekly reading responses and discussion prompts
- two applied lab notebooks (one per module) assessed on **ANUBIS**
  (Android NetHunter Unified Breach Intelligence System) at
  <https://anubis.castalia.institute> — see `anubis.md`
- a capstone analytic memo synthesizing both modules

## Course Rhythm

Each module in this book includes:

- an overview
- prose chapter content
- an assignment page (Thebe-enabled inside the book)
- slides (RISE-compatible notebook)
- narration for the slides
- instructor notes
- a notebook lab assessed on **ANUBIS** (NetHunter + AI; not NYU *Anubis LMS*)
